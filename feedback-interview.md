# User Interview — MayMay's Latt Swal

- **Who:** My mother (daily home cook, prepares Myanmar meals for a family of 4)
- **When:** 2026-07-10
- **How:** In person at home, ~20 min while she was deciding what to cook for lunch

## What they do today (without your project)

She opens Facebook and scrolls through Myanmar cooking groups to find ideas, or asks neighbors what they are cooking. If she already knows what to make, she just goes to the market and buys ingredients based on instinct. She says the hardest part is "figuring out what to cook when I'm tired of the same rotation" — she estimates she repeats about 5–6 dishes every week.

## What they liked

- The random suggestion button ("ဒီနေ့ ဘာချက်ရမလဲ") — she tapped it and immediately got 3 options she hadn't thought of in a while, like ချဉ်ပေါင်ဟင်းချို and ငါးချဉ်ရည်ဟင်း
- Ingredient-based search — she typed "ကြက်ဥ, ခရမ်းချဉ်သီး" and the app suggested ကြက်ဥခရမ်းချဉ်သီးချက် immediately. She said "this is exactly what I need when I open the fridge and don't know what to do with what's there"
- The step-by-step cooking checklist — she liked being able to tick off steps as she cooks, especially for harder recipes like အမဲသားအာလူးဟင်း
- Myanmar-first interface — all text is in Burmese, no English mixed in, which she found comfortable

## What confused them / what's missing

- When she typed "ကြက်သား" (chicken) alone, the search returned nothing because the recipe names use "ကြက်သားအာလူးဟင်း" and the matching logic didn't catch partial ingredient matches well enough — she expected to see all chicken dishes
- She wanted to filter by cooking time ("30 မိနစ်အတွင်းချက်လို့ရတာ ဘာရှိလဲ") but there is no time filter
- She asked if she could save her favorite recipes — "I want to come back to the ones I liked"
- The app felt a bit plain visually — she said "Facebook cooking groups have nicer food photos, this one is all text"

## What would make them actually use it

- More recipes — she said 30 is a good start but she'd need at least 100+ to cover daily cooking
- A "quick meals under 20 minutes" filter — that's what she looks for most on busy days
- Better ingredient matching — if she types any one ingredient, it should show all recipes containing that ingredient
- Visual food images next to each recipe card

## What I'll change (next steps)

- [ ] Improve ingredient matching to handle partial/broad queries (e.g. "ကြက်သား" should match all chicken dishes)
- [ ] Add a cooking time filter (quick / medium / slow)
- [ ] Expand the recipe dataset from 30 to 100+ Myanmar recipes
- [ ] Add recipe images or emoji-based visual indicators per recipe
- [ ] Consider a favorites/bookmark feature for returning users
